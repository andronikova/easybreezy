EasyBreezy is made to help you easily distribute your monthly income by a three simple steps:

1. Fill in your income and, if necessary, update monthly expenses and current value of your accounts.

2. EasyBreezy distributes your money using its algorithm.

3. (optional) You can correct payments by hand, if needed, and save all results in history, if you want.

Account types

There are three types of account in EasyBreezy: expenses, savings and goals. They are different from each other by a strategy to calculate monthly payments as well as by priority in which you distribute your money between them.

Expenses are the payments with fixed monthly sum. For example, you can use this type of account to take into consideration apartment rent or travel card expenses.


Saving accounts are for saving up some money. It is payments you would like to make regularly and the value of payment is some fraction of you income. You also can put upper limit for your savings. Monthly payments will stop as soon as you accumulate this sum. You could use saving accounts to make some emergency savings (for job loss) or nest-egg for retiremenet.

We will ask you to choose one reserve account between your savings. This account will be used in case your income can't cover your expenses. See section "account priority" to read about this situation.


Goal accounts are for saving up for special goal. You should know the date and the amount of money you would like to save by the date. Monthly payment will be calculated by taking into account missed sum and deadline. For example, you would like to use goal account to save up for summer vacation or for some expensive purchase (e.g. car).


Account priority

Account priority is important when your income is not enough to cover all your payments. In this situation you should choose - which accounts you want to replenish first.

In EasyBreezy expenses accounts have the highest priority. It means, that at first income goes to expenses. If income is not enough to cover all your expenses, money from reserve account would be used. In this situation there would be no replenishes for saving and goal accounts.

If after repayment of expenses there are non-zero remains in income, the saving accounts are replenished. If remains are less than total sum of savings payments, remains are distributed between all savings in proportion.

Goals are replenished in the end. If remains are not enough to cover all goals, they are distributed between all goals in proportion.

It could happen that after all you still have non-zero remains. In this case you may add it to any account you wish.
